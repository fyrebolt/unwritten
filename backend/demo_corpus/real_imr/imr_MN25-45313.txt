Findings:  The physician reviewer found that Nature of Statutory Criteria/Case Summary: A patient has requested authorization and coverage for a whole body positron emission tomography (PET) with computed tomography (CT) scan. In the setting of suspected occult malignancy and potential paraneoplastic syndrome, a PET/CT can be used to screen for malignancy. The most commonly detected occult malignancies include lung, brain, and colorectal. Identification of a primary tumor may enable more specific and effective treatment and change clinical management. In one study of suspected malignancy in 175 patients, PET/CT identified a primary site of malignancy in 100 cases. In this case, the patient's hypercalcemia may be indicative of a paraneoplastic syndrome and therefore an underlying malignancy. For these reasons, the requested whole body PET with CT scan is medically necessary for the evaluation of this patient.
