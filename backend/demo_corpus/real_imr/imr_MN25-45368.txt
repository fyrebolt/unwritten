Findings:  The physician reviewer found that Nature of Statutory Criteria/Case Summary: A patient has requested authorization and coverage for Zepbound. According to a study, obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) is characterized by disordered breathing during sleep and excess adiposity is an etiologic risk factor. The authors noted that the benefit of substantial weight reduction in the treatment of patients with OSA is well recognized, and that clinical guidelines recommend treatment of obesity in patients with OSA. The authors studied the use of Zepbound in patients with obesity and moderate-to-severe OSA and found that Zepbound showed significant weight reduction and improvement in the severity of OSA. In this case, the patient presents with obesity based on a body mass index (BMI) of 34.02 kg/m2 and moderate OSA based on an apnea-hypopnea index (AHI) of 17.8. The medical literature supports the use of Zepbound in this patient’s clinical setting. Therefore, the requested Zepbound is medically necessary for the treatment of this patient’s sleep apnea.Final Result: The reviewer determined that the requested medication is medically necessary for the treatment of the patient's medical condition.
